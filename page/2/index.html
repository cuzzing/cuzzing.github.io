《茶花女》摘抄

茶花女摘抄

1

她的头样很美，是一件绝妙的珍品，它长得小巧玲珑，就像缪塞所说的那样，她母亲好像是有意让它生得这么小巧，以便把它精心雕琢一番。

在一张流露着难以描绘其风韵的鹅蛋脸上，嵌着两只乌黑的大眼睛，上面两道弯弯细长的眉毛，纯净得犹如人工画就的一般，眼睛上盖着浓密的睫毛，当眼帘低垂时，给玫瑰色的脸颊投去一抹淡淡的阴影；细巧而挺直的鼻子透出股灵气，鼻翼微鼓，像是对情欲生活的强烈渴望；一张端正的小嘴轮廓分明，柔唇微启，露出一口洁白如奶的牙齿；皮肤颜色就像未经人手触摸过的蜜桃上的绒衣：这些就是这张美丽的脸蛋给您的大致印象。

黑玉色的头发，不知是天然的还是梳理成的，像波浪一样地卷曲着，在额前分梳成两大绺，一直拖到脑后，露出两个耳垂，耳垂上闪烁着两颗各值四五千法郎的钻石耳环。

2

孩子虽然有效，但他是未来的成人；脑袋虽然狭窄，但它蕴藏着无限的思想；眼珠儿才不过一丁点儿大，它却可以看到广阔的天地。

3

赢得一颗没有谈过恋爱的心，这就等于进入一个没有设防的城市。

4

我希望自己能像一个百万富翁似地爱您，但我力不从心；您希望我能够像一个穷光蛋似地爱您，我却又不是那么一无所有。

5

我们就像沉没在爱河之中的两个顽强的潜水员，只是在换气的时候才浮出水面。

6

一个人心中没有爱情的时候可以满足于虚荣，但一旦有了爱情，虚荣就变得庸俗不堪了。

Sleep for only 4-5 hours daily

sleep for only 4-5 hours daily

in so far as/insofar as/in as far as
 to the degree that

The research suggests that the drug will be successful, in so far as one can draw conclusions from such a small sample size.

normalize
 a situation becomes normal again

Journalists are reporting that the situation has now normalized.
Relations between the countries were formally normalized in 1997.

predispose
 make someone more likely to suffer from a particular health problem

predispose sb to sth
Diabetes predisposes patients to infections.

 make someone more likely to behave or think in a particular way

predispose sb to sth/doing sth
Parents who smoke predispose children to smoking.

 n. predisposition [+to/towards]

a predisposition towards alcoholism
genetic predisposition 遗传倾向

bias
 a preference for one thing over another, especially an unfair one

political/gender/racial bias
Students were evaluated without bias or favoritism.
[+against/towards/in favor of]
It’s clear that the company has a bias against women and minorities.
on the bias: in a diagonal direction 沿对角线方向

i.e.
 that is

et al
 written after a list of names to mean that other people are also involved in something

correlation
 a connection between two ideas,facts etc, especially when one may be the cause of the other

Researchers found a strong correlation between urban deprivation(lack) and poor health.
strong/high/close/significant correlation

high-profile
 attracting a lot of public attention, usually deliberately

a high-profile public figure

cumulative
 inceasing gradually as more of something is added or happens

Learning is a cumulative process.
cumulative effect(of sth)
Depression is often caused by the cumulative effects of stress and overwork.

crunch
n.

  1. a noise like the sound of something being crushed 嘎吱声

    The only sound was the crunch of tyres on gravel(碎石路).

  2. a difficult situation caused by a lack of something, especially money or time

    cash/budget/financial crunch
    Cost cutting had enabled the organization to survive a previous cash crunch.

  3. the crunch/crunch time an important time,especially one when a difficult decision has to be made

    When it came to the crunch, she couldn’t agree to marry him.

  4. =sit-up 仰卧起坐

v.

  1. 发出嘎吱声

  2. to eat hard food in a way that makes a noise[+on]

    The dog was crunching on a bone.

  3. crunch (the) numbers do a lot of calculations in order to find an answer

    The computer will crunch all the numbers to determine the final score.

gibe/jibe
 嘲笑；一致
